Algorithm
Three-Layer ESC Identifier

The system processes live wheel slip and friction measurements through a cascade of three layers — each firing when conditions are met, always returning a best estimate.

Layer 1 — Always active
Lookup Table
Maps observed peak friction μ_max to a coarse c₂ estimate via linear interpolation over the four surface reference points.
μ_max → c₂_coarse
→
Layer 2 — When ESC gradient available
ESC-Gradient Analytic
Converts the ESC demodulator gradient to the true friction curve gradient, then solves analytically for c₁ and c₃ at any operating point — not just at the peak.
g_esc → c₁, c₃
→
Layer 3 — When slip excitation sufficient
Brent Root-Finding
Uses two points on the friction curve to solve for c₂ via Brent's method (superlinear convergence, guaranteed bracket). Refines the LUT estimate when ABS excitation is wide enough.
|s_probe − s_peak| > δ → c₂_fine
Burckhardt Friction Model
μ(s) = c₁ · (1 − e−c₂|s|) − c₃ · |s|
c₁ — friction amplitude  |  c₂ — curve sharpness (key discriminant)  |  c₃ — slope after peak
